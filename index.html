WeatherPy Analysis Summary

This project was done to get an idea of what the weather is like around the equator. The objective is to visualize the weather of 500+ cities around the globe that are at varying distances from the equator and to see how the certain weather paramenters like Temperature, Humidity, Cloudiness and Wind Speeds change as we get closer to or away from the equator.

We used Python's Numpy library to generate 600 random latitude and logitude values to ensure that we have coordinate samples from all over the globe. Then we used the Citipy library to get the nearest city to each of the coordinates. We cleaned any duplicate cities, and used the OpenWeatherMap API to get the weather data for each city. The weather data wasn't available for some small/remote cities so we skipped them as well. We generated about 600 city coordinates to ensure that after cleanup we have more than 500 cities. The image above shows all the cities as black dots on the map of the world so that we can see the distribution of the city data for our analysis.

Our overall conclusion was that cities closer to the equator are hotter than cities away from the equator with more cities in the south of the equator being warmer. Proximity to the equator does not affect other factors like Humidity, Cloudiness or Wind Speed as much as it affects the Temperature.
